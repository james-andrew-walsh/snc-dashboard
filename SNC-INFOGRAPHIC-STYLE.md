# SNC Infographic Style Guide

**Date:** 2026-04-02
**Context:** Sierra Nevada Construction (SNC) Equipment Tracking

## 1. Core Aesthetic
Practical, grounded, and professional. The visual language should reflect an engineering and construction firm—clear, utilitarian, and structured. It should feel like a high-end whiteboard session or an architectural schematic, not a sci-fi movie.

## 2. What to Avoid (The "AI Default" Tropes)
- No dark mode with glowing neon lines (cyberpunk/sci-fi aesthetics).
- No abstract futuristic holograms, glowing brains, or floating cyber-nodes representing AI.
- No overly complex, messy "tech" backgrounds.

## 3. What to Embrace (The "Practical Engineering" Look)
- **Backgrounds:** Light, matte, or clean whiteboard style.
- **Colors:** Neutral slate grays, crisp whites, and deep industrial blues, with subtle construction-inspired accents (like muted safety orange or amber).
- **Style:** Flat vector illustrations, clear block diagrams, or architectural blueprint styles.
- **Metaphors:** Use standard UI elements, clean maps, dispatch boards, and structural flowcharts. 

## 4. Prompt Engineering Template for Gemini
When generating any future diagrams or infographics for SNC, append this exact style block to the prompt:

> *"Style: clean corporate infographic, flat vector design, light background, matte slate grays with subtle safety orange and industrial blue accents. Engineering schematic or high-quality whiteboard feel. Grounded, clear, and utilitarian. Absolutely no glowing effects, no dark mode sci-fi, and no futuristic cyberpunk elements."*
